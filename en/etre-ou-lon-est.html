Step I

Being where you are

The founding gesture of the whole path — after The Unfolding Now.

We are almost never here

Look at an ordinary day : how many times are you truly in what you are living? Most of the time we are elsewhere — in what should be, in what is missing, in the improved version of ourselves we hope for later on. And the most surprising thing, Almaas says, is that spiritual seeking itself often becomes a way of running away : we chase after states, experiences, peaks — that is, once again, after something other than what is here.

The proposal of this first step is disarmingly simple : being where you are. Not where you would like to be. Not where you believe you should be. Where you are — in this tiredness, this joy, this boredom, this worry, exactly as they give themselves now.

"Reality is not what is usually reflected in our minds." A. H. Almaas, The Unfolding Now, ch. 1

Being real

Being where you are begins with being real : coinciding with yourself, without playing a character — not even the good student of the spiritual path. We all carry inherited images : the one who holds on, the one who doesn't make waves, the one who understands quickly. Being real means noticing the costume the moment we put it on — and sensing what lies underneath, even if it is more fragile, more blurred, less presentable. What is real does not need to be beautiful : it needs to be true.

"Hands off" — non-interference

Then comes the central instruction, the one Almaas places at the heart of the practice : hands off — don't touch your experience. Whatever arises — sadness, irritation, emptiness, tenderness — we do not push it away, we do not hold on to it, we do not improve it. We let it be, and we look at it with curiosity.

Beware of a common misreading : this is not about passivity in life. Almaas offers a very simple image : if you bite into a rotten fruit, spit it out — of course. Acting in the world remains free and necessary. What we stop manipulating is the inner experience : the taste of disappointment that follows, the irritation, the slight disgust — that, we do not chase away, we let it exist for as long as it has something to say. Non-interference is an act of trust : left alone, experience unfolds by itself and reveals what it covers. The Taoists called this wu wei — Almaas rediscovers it through the path of experience.

The two thieves of presence

Two mechanisms, above all, keep tearing us away from what is. The first is comparative judgment : that inner rule which measures every moment against a "better" — it's not calm enough, not deep enough, not advanced enough. The instant we compare, we have already left what is. The second is darker : self-hatred, that force which attacks what we live and what we are. Almaas recognizes in it a hijacked power — the force of being, turned against itself. At this stage, the point is not to defeat them : only to learn to see them when they are at work. Seeing them is already to no longer belong to them entirely. (Step II will devote a whole piece of work to them, with the inner judge.)

What this first step opens

Being where you are seems modest. Yet it is the gesture that all the rest of the path will only deepen : it is by being exactly there that the layers reveal themselves — the judge, the deficiencies, then the qualities of essence. No one can take this step in our place, and no other step can replace it.

Exploration — 15 minutes

Where am I, right now?

In the spirit of the book's "exploration sessions" — to be lived out in writing, alone, without correcting yourself :

  1. Ask yourself : "Where am I, right now?" — and describe what is really here : sensations, mood, tension, expectation. Without improving, without dramatizing.
  2. Notice each time a voice compares ("I should be more…") or corrects. Note it as it is, then return to what is.
  3. To finish : what changes, in the experience itself, from the simple fact of having stayed with it without touching it?

Fifteen minutes of honest writing is enough. There is nothing to succeed at.

Terms from this step in the bilingual glossary : being real, non-interference (hands off), True Nature, inquiry.
