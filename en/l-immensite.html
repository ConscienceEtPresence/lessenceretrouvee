Step VII

The boundless

Beyond the personal : the dimensions of Being.

The threshold

Until now, something like a “self” has walked this path. A self that has met its judge, felt its holes, recovered Strength or Compassion, gradually matured a true individuality. All these realizations, however deep, remain experiences of someone : there was a self, and something was happening to it.

Here, something else begins to give itself — harder to place, because it is no longer quite of that order. It is not one more experience to add to the list. What changes is the very way experience holds itself : the quiet certainty that there must be someone to whom this is happening begins to loosen. Presence continues to be felt, as real as before — but it gradually stops being my presence and becomes, simply, presence. Almaas speaks, in Nondual Love, of this quiet belief that there must be an “owner” of experience : as long as one holds to it, even without knowing it, a separate subject keeps reconstituting itself, facing a world of separate objects. The threshold is this slight loosening. One does not cross it by deciding to cross it : one notices, one day, that it has already been crossed a little.

The formless dimensions

In The Inner Journey Home, his most ample work of synthesis, Almaas describes what is revealed once essence is no longer recognized merely as the ground of this or that personal quality, but as the ground of all that is. He calls this the formless dimensions of Being. We will not attempt a technical catalogue here — the author himself devotes whole chapters to it, with a precise vocabulary that takes time to absorb. Let us simply say it, the way one would describe a landscape rather than a map :

There is a divine love that no longer goes out toward anyone in particular — because there is no longer really a “someone” on the other end to receive it : a sweetness that does not travel from one point to another, but pervades, like ambient light rather than a directed beam.

There is a pure presence, prior to any content : not a thought about existing, but existing itself, felt, needing nothing more for it to be complete.

There is a luminous void — a space at once dark and clear, lacking nothing precisely because it is made of no particular thing.

And there is the Absolute — the ultimate ground, of which nothing very precise can any longer be said, except that everything, it seems, arises from it and returns to it.

The detail of these dimensions can be found in the bilingual glossary : formless dimensions, the Absolute.

From the personal to the boundless

It is important not to oppose personal qualities and the formless dimensions too sharply. In Almaas's work, the passage does not look like an escape from personhood. It is more like a widening of what is obvious: what was first felt as my presence becomes presence; what was lived as heart love becomes loving light with no center; what was an intelligence of inquiry can sometimes become an intelligence of manifestation itself.

1Personal essence remains precious

The formless does not make the Pearl unnecessary. A vast realization that cannot become accurate speech, true relationship, embodied simplicity, or mature action remains unbalanced. The personal is not an obstacle to be left behind once and for all: it is one way Being learns to live humanly.

2Love widens beyond the object

Personal love knows someone. Merging love melts boundaries. Passionate love consumes separation. Nondual love reveals a sweetness that no longer needs to move from a subject toward an object: it is the very field in which subject and object appear.

3The Absolute is not nihilism

When Almaas speaks of absence, night, or nonbeing, he is not celebrating psychological nothingness. He is pointing to a ground deeper than forms and experiences. This ground may feel stripped down, but it is not a loss of meaning: it is what makes freedom from any fixed form possible.

What not to confuse

Formless does not mean “superior to the personal”: the personal must be integrated.

Nondual does not mean “the ordinary world is worthless”: it means forms are not separate from their depth.

Grace does not mean passivity: effort prepares the ground, but realization is not manufactured by egoic will.

Nondual love

Almaas devotes an entire book to this dimension alone, Nondual Love. What he describes there is no longer love between two people — even the purest essential love, the kind that melts hearts toward one another, still supposes two poles. Nondual love has no poles left. It is not directed toward anything : it is, simply, like a quality of the whole universe — a goodness that seeks to convince no one of anything, because it has no need to be believed in order to be true.

Almaas is very concrete about what prevents us from perceiving it : the attachment to being someone separate, to owning one's own experiences, to defending a “shell” — however refined — rather than letting oneself be traversed. This is not a sad renunciation. It is rather the discovery that there is nothing worth holding onto against that sweetness.

The inexhaustible mystery

A question naturally arises : and then what? Once the Absolute has been touched, what more is there? Almaas answers, in the last of the five Diamond Heart books, with a title that already says it all : Inexhaustible Mystery.

"We can know our spiritual purity, but we can never know it completely. We can never say the last word about it, because there is no end to what can be known, and no way to know it from its own perspective." A. H. Almaas, Diamond Heart Book Five: Inexhaustible Mystery, introduction

Almost twenty years later, in Runaway Realization, one of his last books, Almaas takes up this same intuition from another angle : no view — not even the most nondual one, not even that of the Absolute — exhausts reality. He proposes what he calls a view of totality : a gaze wide enough to welcome every perspective without fixing any one of them as the final truth. Realization is therefore not a summit one reaches and then keeps. It is a continuous unfolding, with no set end — what he calls, with a touch of humor, a “runaway realization”, forever escaping whatever one would like to make of it once and for all.

Coming back down into life

None of this has any value if the boundless becomes a new escape — an elsewhere more interesting than the cup of tea in front of us. Almaas insists on this especially in Luminous Night's Journey, that late journal in which he recounts, day after day, how his own realization of the Absolute had to be integrated into a very ordinary life : a partner, friends, a death to come.

There he describes a discovery that surpasses even the most complete mystical union : coemergence. The boundless and the concrete person are no longer lived as two states visited by turns, nor as one containing the other : they arise together, in the same instant, with no hierarchy between them. One can be, all at once and fully, the embodied presence that drinks its tea, grows old, loves particular people — and the silent immensity that has neither age nor edge. Neither erases the other. That is coemergence : not a compromise between the finite and the infinite, but their simultaneous presence, each fully itself.

A horizon, not a program

This much needs to be said clearly : this step does not read like the previous ones. It describes late, dense books, written for readers already long formed in the Diamond Approach — not for a first encounter. What we give here is a faithful glimpse, not a set of instructions.

There is no exercise for “reaching” the boundless : wanting to reach it would already be a misunderstanding of it, since it is separate from nothing. This site will keep moving forward, step after step, toward what it describes here from a distance : consider this page a horizon glimpsed from the valley, not a next destination to check off.

Exploration — for remembering

A moment already lived

Nothing to bring about here : only to remember. Almost everyone has known, at least once, a moment of boundlessness — before the sea, under a very dark night sky, in an unusual silence, or any other moment when space suddenly seemed far vaster than usual.

  1. Let such a memory come, however brief or uncertain. Do not look for the most spectacular one : the truest is enough.
  2. Stay with it a moment, in detail : the light, the air, the body at that moment.
  3. Then simply notice this : in that instant, the moment did not need a busy “self” to be complete. It already was, before anyone thought to ask who was living it.

Nothing to conclude from it. It has already happened — that is enough for today.
