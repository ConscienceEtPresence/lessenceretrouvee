Guided entry

Discovery Path

A doorway in several rhythms: feel first, understand next, and deepen without losing the living texture of experience.

This path is not a reduced summary of Almaas. It offers a first crossing, as one might enter a large house through a clear doorway before visiting its more hidden rooms. The idea is simple: visitors can skim, then return to the same place with more precision, vocabulary, practice and references.

The Diamond Approach is not merely a system of ideas. It teaches another way of looking at immediate experience: lack as a clue to a quality, defense as an intelligible protection, the question as a living movement, understanding as a transformation of perception.

Choose your rhythm

Overview — 7 min

Read this page straight through to sense the general logic: lack, defense, inquiry, essential qualities.

Understand — 30 min

Open the four main doors: holes, practice, talks, then the library.

Deepen — visits

Return through lexicons, diagrams and talks: each notion becomes a more precise entry point.

Three entries according to your need

Not everyone arrives at this site with the same expectation. Some want only to understand who Almaas is. Others are looking for a practice. Others want to enter the books term by term. The discovery path therefore needs several thresholds, without sacrificing depth.

I am discovering

Begin with the 12 notions, then the path. The aim is to sense the overall thread without getting lost in vocabulary.

I want to practice

Go toward Being where you are, the inner judge, and practice. The aim is to transform a real experience, not simply read more.

I want to study

Move through Deepening Almaas, the library, and the lexicons. The aim is to connect each notion with its source books.

What this path should let you feel

A first visit does not need to explain everything, but it should already change something in perception. In Almaas, a difficulty is not only a problem to solve: it may express a quality lost from view. A defense is not only an error: it may protect a wound that has never been understood. A question is not only mental: it can become a way of remaining present to what is revealing itself.

This is the thread of the path: start from lived experience, do not jump too quickly to a beautiful idea, recognize the psychological structure, then ask which essential quality is involved. The hurried visitor can remain here. The visitor who truly wants to understand can then open Deepening Almaas and follow the same notions in greater detail.

Small marker — before choosing a door

Turning a sentence into a path

Take the sentence that brings you here: “I judge myself too much,” “I lack strength,” “I don't know who I am,” “I need love,” “I want to understand the enneagram.” In Almaas's spirit, that sentence is not merely a theme. It already contains a structure, a possible wound, a defense, and perhaps an essential quality to recover.

  1. If the sentence speaks of lack, open The holes.
  2. If it speaks of inner attack, open The inner judge.
  3. If it speaks of identity or the need for confirmation, open The Point.
  4. If it speaks of love, dependency, or projection, open The path of love.

The path in six doors

I — Start from lack

What is missing may point to what is lost from view

The theory of holes gives a decisive reversal: a painful emptiness is not only a psychological deficit; it can be the trace of an essential quality cut off from consciousness.

 II — Meet the defense

The inner judge also protects what it attacks

The superego is not only a harsh voice to silence. It often maintains an old organization of safety. Understanding it makes it possible not to obey it blindly.

 III — Learn inquiry

The question becomes a way of inhabiting experience

Inquiry is not mental analysis: it is an open, precise and embodied exploration of what is alive now, carried by love of truth and Diamond Guidance.

 IV — Recognize the qualities

Essence appears through many faces

Strength, compassion, will, peace, joy, love, value, brilliancy: each quality answers a sector of experience and illuminates a particular wound.

 V — Become real in life

The Pearl does not erase the person; it makes the person real

Realization is not an escape from the human. Almaas emphasizes integration: becoming a person inhabited by Being, capable of autonomy, relationship and real action.

 VI — Return to the books

The site orients; the books work in depth

The library, lexicons and talks help readers choose a book by need: void, love, identity, enneagram, inquiry, intelligence or dimensions of Being.
First inquiry gesture — 10 minutes

Moving from theme to experience

  1. Choose a simple sentence: “I lack strength”, “I want to be seen”, “I cannot trust”.
  2. Do not look for the explanation right away. Locate the sentence's effect in the body.
  3. Ask gently: what do I believe I will lose if I stay with this? what am I trying to obtain from outside?
  4. Notice the defense: judgment, distraction, justification, fog, mental acceleration.
  5. End with a provisional truth, not a final one: “for now, I see that...”.

This gesture is not meant to produce a beautiful conclusion. It helps move from idea to sensation, then from sensation to living understanding.
