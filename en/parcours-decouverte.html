Simple entry

Discovery Path

An invitation to enter Almaas's work through living experience, without a school-like detour.

Almaas's work is not first discovered as a system to memorize. It is met in a very simple question: what is happening now, if I stop for a moment fleeing, correcting, explaining, or improving my experience?

This is why the site's path begins with Being where you are. It enters through the nearest lived experience, then gradually discovers the inner judge, the holes, essence, its qualities, the Pearl, the Point, and the wider dimensions of Being.

The underlying movement

In Almaas, a difficulty is not only a problem to solve. It may reveal a way of being cut off from Being. A defense is not only an error: it often protects a wound or a quality that has become inaccessible. Inquiry therefore does not look for a quick answer; it learns to remain in contact with reality until a deeper understanding reveals itself.

Conceptual landmarks exist, but they come later. The 12 key notions, the glossary, and the library help return and clarify what the path has already made living.

First threshold

Begin with what is here

Before seeking a great spiritual experience, Almaas invites us to look precisely at immediate experience: a tension, a shame, an expectation, a closing, an aspiration, a softness already present. The path begins there, not in an idea of what we should become.

Working sources: local syntheses of Diamond Heart, Essence, The Pearl Beyond Price, Spacecruiser Inquiry and book-by-book lexicons. The path remains a pedagogical map of the site, not an official order of the Diamond Approach.
